Clear, printable bus maps for your community

We help local organisations publish easy-to-read maps of the buses in their area — or the buses that reach their doorstep — and keep them up to date as services change.

Two kinds of map

Choose whichever fits — or have both.

Area

A whole place

A town, a rural parish, or part of a larger town — every bus within the area, drawn to scale with local landmarks.

Place

A single point

Centred on one spot — a shop, school, station, community centre or town centre — showing the buses that serve it and where they go.

Four outputs — you choose which you want

Each map can produce any combination of these. Turn on only what you need.

🗺️

Internal — geographic

Street-anchored map of the buses within the area or around the place.

📐

Internal — schematic

A straightened, tidied version that’s easy to follow at a glance.

🚇

Internal — diagram

A tube-map-style diagram of the local network.

↗️

External

Where the buses go — a diagram of routes to their destinations.

How it works

Apply & get approved

Tell us who you are and what you’d like. We approve your organisation and your first area or places.

We set it up

We build your map from official open data and check it carefully.

You fine-tune

Adjust colours and labels, choose which outputs you want, and preview.

Approve & print

Every map is signed off before it goes out. Download a print-ready A4 sheet — and accept updates as services change.

Made to be trusted

✅

Human sign-off

People rely on these to catch a bus, so a person checks and approves every map before it can be printed.

📶

Official open data

Built from the UK Bus Open Data Service and OpenStreetMap, refreshed monthly.

🎨

Readable by design

Colour-blind-safe route colours and generous type, sized for real printing.
