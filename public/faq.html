Frequently asked questions

Can’t see your question? Ask us — we’re happy to help.

What is Community Bus Maps?

A service that produces clear, printable bus maps for a place — a whole town or parish (an area map), or a single point such as a shop, school or centre (a place map) — and helps you keep them current as services change.

Who can apply?

Any organisation whose community uses the bus: councils, shops, businesses, schools, event organisers, charities, attractions and more. Every application is reviewed and approved before an account is set up.

How much does it cost?

Nothing at the moment. We may introduce charges in future, but always with notice — you would never be billed by surprise.

How many maps can I have?

To start with, each customer can have one area and a few places. Each map is approved individually. We expect to raise these limits over time.

What can I change myself?

You can choose which outputs you want, recolour and relabel routes, turn landmarks on or off, and accept the monthly updates — then download print-ready files. The more delicate layout work (straightening lines, nudging labels) we do for you, so the result always looks right.

What are the four outputs?

An internal geographic map (drawn to the streets), an internal schematic (straightened and tidied), an internal diagram (tube-map style), and an external map showing where the buses go. You pick which ones you want for each map.

How up to date are the maps?

We refresh from official data every month. When something changes on your routes, you’ll see “a change to review”, preview it, and accept it — your colours and labels carry over automatically.

Where does the data come from?

Bus routes, stops and timetables come from the UK Bus Open Data Service (BODS), published under the Open Government Licence. Streets, rivers and landmarks come from OpenStreetMap (© OpenStreetMap contributors, ODbL). Live times should always be checked with the operator or at bustimes.org.

Can I print them?

Yes — every map is produced as a print-ready A4 sheet at 300 dpi (and as an SVG), sized and coloured for real printing.

Do you guarantee they’re correct?

A person reviews and signs off every map before it can be printed, and we build only from official open data. But timetables change; these maps are a guide, so please encourage readers to confirm live times before travelling.

What do you do with my details?

We use them only to consider your application and to set up and support your maps. We don’t sell them or use them for anything else, and you can ask us to delete them.
